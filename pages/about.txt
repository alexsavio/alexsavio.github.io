I'm **Alexandre Manhães Savio**, a freelance Software, Cloud and AI engineer.
I help R&D-heavy teams ship reliable systems — the kind of work where the
problem isn't yet well defined, the data is messy, and the product has to
survive contact with real users.

## Background

I spent more than eight years in academia working on brain image processing
and machine learning for medical analysis. After a few post-docs and a
startup, I made the jump out of research and into industry — first through
consultancies, working with large European companies on robotics, data
analytics and engineering, and cloud solutions. Today I work independently
as a freelancer.

I'm comfortable anywhere on the stack a data-intensive product needs:
designing cloud architectures, building ingestion and transformation
pipelines, wiring up ML systems, and writing the production code that ties
them together end-to-end.

## How I work

- **Small engagements, senior judgment.** I work best embedded in a small
  team where I can own a problem end-to-end rather than sit in a staffing
  pool.
- **First principles, not frameworks.** My [writing](/archives.html)
  reflects how I think about projects: strip the hype, find the irreducible
  constraints, then pick tools proportional to the actual problem.
- **Long-form partnerships.** Most of my engagements run for months, not
  days. I'm not the right fit for two-week staff augmentation.
