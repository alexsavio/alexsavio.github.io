## What I take on

I'm most useful on engagements where the hard part isn't the code, it's
the reasoning around the problem:

- **Cloud & data platforms** — designing and building the infrastructure,
  ingestion, and transformation layers for data-intensive products.
- **AI / ML integration** — wiring models into real systems, with a clear
  view of what's production-grade and what's still research.
- **Technical leadership on early-stage products** — acting as a software
  architect alongside a founding team, owning the system design and the
  non-obvious trade-offs.
- **Research-to-production bridges** — translating work from scientific
  codebases into something a product team can actually operate.

Languages I reach for most: **Python, Rust, C++, SQL, bash, TypeScript**
when needed. Cloud: **AWS, GCP, Azure** at varying depths. Data:
**Postgres, object stores, batch and streaming pipelines, distributed
systems** when the workload genuinely demands it. ML: enough to build,
evaluate, and deploy — I'm an engineer who ships ML, not a researcher
who publishes it. AI: **LLMs, agents, RAG pipelines**, treated as
engineering problems with real evaluation and real failure modes, not
as demos.

## What I don't take on

Being clear about this saves everyone time:

- **Pure staff augmentation** at large consultancies.
- **WordPress, Shopify, marketing sites, mobile apps.**
- **Ad-tech** projects.
- **Fixed-price fixed-scope** quotes on genuinely novel work. I'm happy
  to do fixed-price on well-defined increments inside a larger engagement.

## How an engagement usually starts

1. **Intro call (30 min, free).** We talk about what you're trying to
   build and whether I'm the right fit. If I'm not, I'll say so and often
   I can point you somewhere better.
2. **Scoped discovery (paid, 1–2 weeks).** A short, paid piece of work
   where I dig into the code, data, or architecture and come back with a
   written plan, risks, and a proposed engagement shape.
3. **Main engagement.** Usually month-to-month, remote, with a clear
   check-in cadence and a written exit plan from day one.

Rates and contract shape are discussed on the intro call, based on scope
and engagement length.
